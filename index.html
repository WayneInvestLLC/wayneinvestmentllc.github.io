Investment Philosophy

Return matters. Feasibility
comes first.

Every deal we review starts with the same question: can this actually be built, financed, and delivered?

Return is a byproduct of execution. We care about it — but chasing yield on a project that was never truly financeable is how capital gets lost. Our job is to assess whether a deal is grounded in reality before we assess whether it's attractive.

We underwrite the business plan, not the pitch. If the numbers only work under best-case assumptions, we'll say so directly. If the structure is sound and the sponsor can deliver, we move fast.

What we look for
A deal we'd stake capital on, not just approve.
  • Realistic underwriting Assumptions grounded in market evidence, not optimism
  • Financeable structure Senior debt in place or clearly achievable
  • Experienced sponsor A track record of delivering comparable projects
  • Clear exit path A defined strategy — sale, refinance, or absorption
What matters most
The project has to work before returns can.
  • Deliverability Can the project be completed on time and on budget?
  • Sponsor alignment Do we trust how this team operates under pressure?
  • Rational return We don't need extraordinary — we need achievable
